Hi, I'm Emiko

I am a second-year graduate student at UC Berkeley, where I work with Prof. Luke Z. Kelley researching low-frequency gravitational waves from supermassive black hole binaries (SMBHBs). When I'm not coding in my favorite coffee shop, I also enjoy lifting, rugby, and the occassional jog.

nHz Gravitational Waves

  • Continuous Waves

    SMBHBs at small separations are theorized to produce continuous gravitational waves (CWs) at nanoHertz frequencies. The superposition of many of these CWs results in a stochastic gravitational wave background (GWB), for which current pulsar timing arrays (PTAs) have found strong evidence. I semi-analytically model populations of SMBHBs to forecast single source detections, depending on astrophysical models.

  • GWB Anisotropy

    Before individual CW sources are detected, scientists expect to measure louder and quieter regions of the GW sky deviation from a completely isotropic background. This is GW Anisotropy, and has not been statistically-significantly detected yet. With holodeck, I calculate the level of anisotropy from simulated SMBHB populations through spherical harmonic decomposition, and have found median predictions to fall among current upper limits (Agazie et al. 2023).

Past Astronomy Research

  • Ionization in Massive Protostellar Outflows

    I developed a post-processing pipeline to model shock and photo ionization in simulated massive protostellar outflows. These serve as likely mechanisms behind the strong radio emissions visible from massive protostellar outflows. I worked on this as an undergraduate research fellow in the Virginia Initiative on Cosmic Origins with Jonathan Tan, Jan Staff, and Jon Ramsey.

    • Learn More

  • X-Shaped Radio Galaxies Hosting SMBHBs

    X-shaped radio galaxies are a rare phenomenon in which galaxies with bright radio lobes have a second set of dimmer radio-visible wings, forming and X-shape. One scenario to explain these wings is that they are relic emissions from a previous spin orientation, which could have been reoriented due to binary interactions between dual SMBHs. Under the advisorship of Ilsang Yoon and Bjorn Emonts, at the National Radio Astronomy Observatory, I searched for signatures of binary SMBHBs among all known X-shaped Radio Galaxies.
